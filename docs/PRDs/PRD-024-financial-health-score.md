# PRD-024 — Financial Health Score

**Priority**: P2
**Status**: Draft
**Registry**: [REGISTRY.md](./REGISTRY.md)

---

## Problem Statement

Credit scores only measure creditworthiness, not overall financial health. Users lack a single, holistic metric that considers savings rate, emergency fund status, debt-to-income ratio, subscription burden, bill payment reliability, and yield optimization. A comprehensive score gives users a clear picture of how well their money is working and what to improve.

---

## Key Ideas

- Multi-factor scoring model (savings, debt, bills, yield, subscriptions)
- Component breakdown showing contribution of each factor
- Trend tracking over time (is your score improving?)
- Personalized improvement recommendations ranked by impact
- Anonymized peer benchmarking (how do you compare?)
- Score simulation ("if you cancel X, your score goes up Y points")
- Gamification elements to drive engagement with improvement actions

---

## Success Metrics

- Score check frequency (how often users view their score)
- Recommendation action rate (% of suggestions acted on)
- Average score improvement over 90 days
- Correlation between score improvement and real financial outcomes
- Feature virality (users sharing or discussing their score)
