# PRD-019 — Category Budget Guardrails

**Priority**: P1
**Status**: Draft
**Registry**: [REGISTRY.md](./REGISTRY.md)

---

## Problem Statement

Traditional budgets are rigid and punishing. Users set budgets, break them by day 12, and give up entirely. Orbit should offer flexible "guardrails" — soft limits with agent-powered nudges rather than hard stops. The agent adapts to real life instead of shaming users, making budgeting feel supportive rather than restrictive.

---

## Key Ideas

- Category spending limits as soft guardrails (not hard blocks)
- Real-time pace tracking ("you're 80% through dining budget on day 15")
- Agent nudges when approaching limits with actionable suggestions
- Automatic rebalancing between categories (underspent groceries covers dining)
- Rollover of unused budget to next period
- "Guilt-free" discretionary fund for spontaneous spending
- Historical trend-based budget recommendations

---

## Success Metrics

- Budget adherence rate (% of months within guardrails)
- User engagement with nudges (acknowledged vs. dismissed)
- Month-over-month spending reduction in target categories
- Budget abandonment rate vs. traditional budgeting tools
- User-reported financial stress reduction
