# PRD-037 — Group Bill Negotiation

**Priority**: P3
**Status**: Draft
**Registry**: [REGISTRY.md](./REGISTRY.md)

---

## Problem Statement

Individual negotiation power is limited. But if 10,000 Orbit users all have the same internet provider, collective bargaining power is significant. Group negotiation could unlock savings that no individual could achieve alone.

---

## Key Ideas

- Provider cohort identification (users sharing the same provider)
- Collective bargaining campaigns when critical mass is reached
- Group switching leverage for competitive provider markets
- Anonymized participation to protect user privacy
- Savings sharing model (how group discounts are distributed)
- Provider partnership deals negotiated on behalf of user cohorts
- Critical mass thresholds that trigger negotiation attempts
- Opt-in privacy controls at every step

---

## Success Metrics

- Cohort size per provider (users eligible for group negotiation)
- Campaign launch rate (% of cohorts reaching critical mass)
- Average savings per user per successful negotiation
- Opt-in rate for group negotiation campaigns
- Provider response/acceptance rate
