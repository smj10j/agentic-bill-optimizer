# PRD-029 — Savings Challenges & Gamification

**Priority**: P2
**Status**: Draft
**Registry**: [REGISTRY.md](./REGISTRY.md)

---

## Problem Statement

Saving money is psychologically hard. Gamification — challenges, streaks, milestones, and social competition — makes saving feel rewarding rather than restrictive. The agent should turn financial improvement into a game users want to play.

---

## Key Ideas

- 30-day savings challenges with configurable goals
- No-spend day streaks and streak recovery mechanics
- Savings milestones with celebrations and unlock moments
- "Beat your last month" self-competitions
- Opt-in, anonymized leaderboards for social motivation
- Achievement badges for financial behaviors
- Challenge templates (no-eat-out week, subscription diet, etc.)
- Reward mechanisms tied to real savings progress

---

## Success Metrics

- Challenge participation rate (% of active users who join a challenge)
- Challenge completion rate
- Incremental savings attributable to challenges vs. non-participants
- Streak length distribution and retention lift among challenge users
- Monthly active engagement frequency (sessions/week)
