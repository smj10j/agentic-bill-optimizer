# PRD-014 — Emergency Fund Autopilot

**Priority**: P1
**Status**: Draft
**Registry**: [REGISTRY.md](./REGISTRY.md)

---

## Problem Statement

56% of Americans can't cover a $1,000 emergency expense. Building an emergency fund feels impossible when money is tight because it requires conscious sacrifice. The agent should invisibly build an emergency fund through micro-savings and smart allocation so users accumulate a safety net without feeling the pinch.

---

## Key Ideas

- **Target calculation**: recommend 3-6 months of expenses based on user's actual spending
- **Micro-sweep strategy**: move small amounts ($1-5) frequently rather than large amounts rarely
- **Round-up contributions**: round purchases to nearest dollar and sweep the difference
- **Windfall detection**: automatically allocate portions of tax refunds, bonuses, and large deposits
- **Progress tracking**: clear visualization of goal progress with projected completion date
- **Emergency withdrawal**: instant access, no penalty, no friction when actually needed
- **Milestone celebrations**: positive reinforcement at $100, $500, $1000 milestones

---

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Users with active emergency fund goal | >40% |
| Avg monthly contribution (micro-savings) | >$50 |
| Users reaching $1,000 milestone within 6 months | >30% |
| Emergency fund withdrawal and replenish rate | >80% replenish within 60 days |
| User-reported financial confidence improvement | Measurable lift in survey |
